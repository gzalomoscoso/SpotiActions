❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Personal Playlists & Library Mapping

How do I add my own personal playlists as options to be chosen?

Due to background execution restrictions between iOS sandboxing and Spotify, third-party apps cannot resolve dynamic custom URLs on the fly in the background without forcing a manual confirmation alert. SpotiActions circumvents this rule using pre-defined pipeline layouts located at the base of the app dashboard, identified by a distinct orange indicator (such as WakeUp Happy, Daily Mix, Modo Booster, Happy Hits, or My Liked Songs).

To bind your custom audio tracks, simply navigate down to the orange layout rows, launch your favorite collection through a chosen profile row at least once to fulfill the security handshake, and then select that corresponding profile name within your iOS Shortcuts parameter list.

Do I need to grant profile permissions every single time?

No. Initial playback synchronization through an orange-indicated slot acts as a one-time structural initialization. Once the authorization footprint is mapped inside iOS, your automated routines will execute seamlessly without ever asking for verification again.

Technical Troubleshooting

Shortcuts not working or failing to execute properly?

To avoid background process collision, clear any legacy files. Access your native Shortcuts library directory, wipe any duplicate, older, or corrupted SpotiActions routines, and verify that only a single instance of SpotiActions303 is live. Open the utility interface to cleanly deploy a fresh configuration copy if required.

App not launching Spotify?

Confirm the official Spotify platform binary is active on your device environment and logged into a functioning user account profile. SpotiActions functions strictly as an endpoint automation management bridge.
